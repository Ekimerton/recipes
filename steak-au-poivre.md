# Steak au Poivre

This classic French bistro favorite features tender beef medallions coated in a thick crust of coarsely cracked black peppercorns. Seared to perfection, the steaks are served with a velvety, rich pan sauce crafted from cognac, shallots, and heavy cream. It is a wonderfully elegant yet rustic dish that perfectly balances fiery pepper with luxurious cream.

- **Source:** [Original Recipe](https://www.seriouseats.com/steak-au-poivre)

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless beef medallions, such as filet mignon (about 6 to 8 ounces each)
- Kosher salt, to taste
- 1 ounce of whole black peppercorns (divided)
- 3 tablespoons of neutral cooking oil (like canola or vegetable oil)
- 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter
- 2 fresh thyme sprigs
- 1 medium garlic clove, peeled
- 1/2 of a large shallot, finely minced (roughly 1.5 ounces)
- 2 tablespoons of cognac or quality brandy
- 3/4 cup of chicken stock (low-sodium or homemade)
- 3/4 cup of heavy whipping cream (or 6 tablespoons of crème fraîche)
- 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard

## Instructions

1. **Prep the Beef**: Season the medallion steaks generously on all sides with kosher salt. Arrange them on a wire rack set over a rimmed baking sheet and let them air-dry uncovered in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.
2. **Crush the Peppercorns**: Coarsely crack the whole peppercorns. You can use a mortar and pestle, or place them in a plastic bag and crush them using a heavy skillet or rolling pin. Ensure they remain in coarse, rustic pieces rather than a fine powder.
3. **Form the Crust**: Heat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Press one flat side of each medallion firmly into the cracked pepper to create a solid, even crust. Place them pepper side up, keeping any leftover pepper to the side.
4. **Sear the Steaks**: In a heavy skillet (like cast iron), heat the neutral oil over medium-high until it is shimmering. Carefully lay the steaks in the pan, pepper-crust side down, and cook for 3 minutes to toast the pepper. Carefully flip the steaks over, then add the unsalted butter, thyme sprigs, and garlic clove. Use a spoon to baste the steaks with the melted butter as they continue to sear.
5. **Finish in the Oven**: Transfer the steaks onto a clean baking sheet and roast in the oven until they reach an internal temperature of 125°F (52°C) for medium-rare, or cook to your preferred level of doneness. Remove and let the beef rest on a plate for 5 minutes.
6. **Build the Cream Sauce**: Pour off the excess grease from the pan, leaving about a tablespoon of fat, and throw away the garlic and thyme. Reduce the heat to medium and stir in the minced shallots and the remaining cracked pepper, cooking for 2 minutes until softened. Turn off the flame (especially if using gas), pour in the cognac or brandy, and simmer until it has almost completely evaporated. Add the chicken stock and bring to a simmer, scraping up any flavorful browned bits from the pan. Pour in the heavy cream (or crème fraîche) and simmer until the sauce reduces and thickens enough to coat a spoon. Turn off the heat and whisk in the Dijon mustard, then season with salt to taste.
7. **Serve**: Place the rested steaks onto individual plates and spoon the warm, velvety pan sauce generously over them.

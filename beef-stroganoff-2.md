# Beef Stroganoff

This ultimate version of the classic beef stroganoff features perfectly seared, juicy beef, caramelized pearl onions, and savory mushrooms, all coated in an exceptionally rich, velvety sour cream sauce enhanced with umami-rich ingredients like fish sauce, soy sauce, and Worcestershire.

- **Source:** [Original Recipe](https://www.seriouseats.com/ultimate-beef-stroganof-recipe)

## Ingredients

### Seasoned Steak
- 1.5 to 2 lbs beef steak, any cut (such as tenderloin, sirloin, ribeye, or flank steak)
- 1 tbsp mild sweet paprika
- 1.5 tbsp neutral cooking oil (such as canola or vegetable)
- Coarse kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper, as needed

### Sauce and Vegetables
- 5 tsp unflavored gelatin powder (two 0.25-ounce packets)
- 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth, preferably homemade
- 1.5 tbsp neutral cooking oil (such as canola or vegetable)
- 12 oz white button mushrooms, quartered
- 8 oz pearl onions, peeled
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
- 1 medium shallot, thinly sliced (about 1/4 cup)
- 4 fresh thyme sprigs
- 1 tbsp mild sweet paprika
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp Asian fish sauce
- 2 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 cup dry white wine
- 1 cup full-fat sour cream, plus more for serving

### Garnish
- 1/4 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

## Instructions

### Prepare Broth and Beef

1. In a liquid measuring cup, sprinkle the gelatin powder over the chicken broth and allow it to bloom.
2. Whisk together 1 tablespoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of black pepper, and 1 tablespoon of the sweet paprika in a small bowl. Generously season all sides of the beef with this spice blend, pressing it with your hands to make sure it adheres.
3. Heat the neutral cooking oil designated for the beef in a large skillet or sauté pan over high heat until it just begins to smoke. Sear the beef, flipping it every 20 to 30 seconds, until a deep brown crust develops and the center registers 115°F for rare or 125°F for medium-rare (adjust cooking time depending on the thickness of your cut). Transfer the beef to a clean plate and set aside.

### Sauté Veggies and Aromatics

1. Add the remaining cooking oil to the same skillet. Add the quartered button mushrooms and lower the heat to medium. Cook, stirring regularly, until they release their liquid and begin to brown, approximately 8 minutes.
2. Add 1 tablespoon of butter and the peeled pearl onions to the skillet. Continue to cook, stirring frequently, until both the mushrooms and onions are tender and deeply browned, about 6 minutes.
3. Stir in the sliced shallots, fresh thyme sprigs, and the remaining 1 tablespoon of paprika. Cook and stir until highly fragrant, about 1 minute.
4. Stir in the soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, fish sauce, and Dijon mustard. Cook for about 30 seconds, stirring constantly, until most of the moisture has evaporated.
5. Pour any collected resting juices from the beef plate into the pan, followed by the dry white wine. Simmer until the wine has reduced down to just a few tablespoons.

### Build the Sauce

1. Pour the chicken stock and gelatin mixture into the pan and bring to a rapid simmer. Carefully pour the hot liquid out of the skillet into a heatproof measuring cup or bowl.
2. Place the sour cream in a medium bowl. While whisking constantly, slowly drizzle the hot liquid over the sour cream, whisking until the mixture is fully smooth and homogeneous.
3. Pour this creamy mixture back into the skillet along with the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Add the beef back to the pan for about 1 minute to warm it through, then remove the pan from the heat.

### Assemble and Serve

1. Transfer the beef from the skillet to a cutting board and slice into thin strips.
2. Stir half of the chopped parsley directly into the warm, creamy mushroom and onion sauce in the skillet.
3. Divide the sliced beef among warm serving plates or bowls and spoon the rich sauce generously over the top. Garnish each serving with a dollop of fresh sour cream, a sprinkle of the remaining parsley, and serve immediately (advise guests to discard the thyme sprigs as they eat).

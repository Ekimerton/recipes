# Seattle-Style Chicken Teriyaki

A classic Pacific Northwest favorite featuring juicy, marinated grilled chicken thighs lacquered in a sweet and savory ginger-garlic glaze, served with a simple sweet-mayo dressed iceberg salad and steamed rice.

- **Source:** [Original Recipe](https://youtu.be/OtZZitPNzGo?is=MAzmF-egVJFgSnNT)

## Ingredients

### Chicken & Teriyaki Sauce
- 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- 1 cup sake
- 1 cup mirin
- 1 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup brown sugar
- 1 small chunk carrot
- 2 whole garlic cloves, peeled
- 2 whole slices fresh ginger
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp cornstarch
- 2 tbsp cold water

### Salad & Dressing
- 1/2 head iceberg lettuce, chopped into 1.5-inch squares
- 1/4 cup shredded carrot
- A small handful of red cabbage, shredded
- 1/2 cup Japanese mayonnaise (such as Kewpie)
- 1/4 cup rice vinegar
- 1/4 cup white sugar
- Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

### For Serving
- Steamed white rice

## Instructions


### Prep the Teriyaki Sauce

1. In a saucepan, combine the sake, mirin, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
2. Add the carrot chunk, whole garlic cloves, ginger slices, minced ginger, minced garlic, and 1 teaspoon of rice vinegar.
3. Reduce the heat and let the mixture simmer for about 10 to 15 minutes to reduce slightly and infuse with the aromatics.

### Marinate the Chicken

1. Remove and discard the large aromatic pieces (whole garlic, ginger slices, and carrot chunk) from the sauce, leaving the minced garlic and ginger behind.
2. Butterfly the chicken thighs to ensure even thickness and uniform cooking.
3. Transfer a few spoonfuls of this unthickened warm sauce into a bowl or zip-top bag with the chicken thighs. Toss to coat and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour (and up to 8 hours).

### Thicken the Sauce

1. In a small bowl, whisk the cornstarch and cold water together until smooth to create a slurry.
2. Bring the remaining sauce in the saucepan back to a simmer. Gradually whisk in the cornstarch slurry and cook until the sauce thickens into a glossy, smooth glaze. Remove from the heat and set aside.

### Prep the Salad and Dressing

1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the Japanese mayonnaise, 1/4 cup rice vinegar, white sugar, and a generous amount of black pepper until the sugar is fully dissolved.
2. In a separate salad bowl, toss together the chopped iceberg lettuce, shredded carrot, and shredded red cabbage.

### Grill and Serve

1. Preheat an outdoor grill or grill pan over high heat and lightly oil the grates.
2. Place the marinated chicken thighs on the grill, smooth-side down. Grill until nicely charred and browned, then flip.
3. Brush the grilled side of the chicken with some of the thickened teriyaki glaze. Continue cooking, flipping and lacquering with the glaze a few times, until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 175°F.
4. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes. Slice the thighs at an angle across the grain into strips.
5. Serve the sliced chicken alongside scoops of steamed white rice and the salad. Drizzle the salad with the sweet mayo dressing, and spoon extra teriyaki glaze over the chicken.
